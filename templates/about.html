About This Site

What is this?

Is My Nail Bar Screwed? is a no-frills tool for finding out whether your favourite nail bar is still standing, teetering on the edge, or has already gone the way of the dodo. We track hundreds of UK nail bars using publicly available business rates data, so you don't turn up clutching your chipped gel polish only to find a boarded-up shopfront.

What is the Screwed Score™?

The Screwed Score is a proprietary (read: we made it up, but we made it up responsibly) risk index from 1 to 100. Higher score = more screwed. It's calculated using a heady cocktail of:

  • 📊 Rateable value vs. estimated turnover — if the rates are eating their lunch, that's a red flag.
  • 🏚️ Local vacancy rate — a nail bar surrounded by empty units is a nail bar on borrowed time.
  • 📅 Last verified open date — the longer ago we checked, the more nervous we are.
  • 😬 Status indicators — "At Risk" means we're sweating. "Closed" means it's over. "Permanently Closed" means pour one out.

A score of 1–30 means they're probably fine — keep booking those appointments. 31–60 is the nervous zone. 61–80 and we'd start making alternative arrangements. 80+ and honestly, maybe ring ahead before you get on the bus.

Where does the data come from?

Business rates data is sourced from the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) — publicly available under the Open Government Licence. Rateable values are the VOA's assessment of a property's annual rental value, used to calculate business rates bills. We combine this with publicly available Companies House data and our own field verification.

⚠️ Disclaimer

This site is for informational purposes only. Data may be out of date, incomplete, or occasionally just wrong — we're doing our best here, not running MI6. Don't make major life decisions based on a Screwed Score. If you run a nail bar and think we've got your info wrong, we'd genuinely love to hear from you.

We don't want nail bars to close. We just think people deserve to know what's going on.
