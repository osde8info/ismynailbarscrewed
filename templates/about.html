About Is My Nail Bar Screwed?

What is this site?

Is My Nail Bar Screwed? helps you find out how hard UK business rates are hitting nail bars in your area. Business rates are a tax on non-domestic properties — and for nail bars operating on tight margins, a high rateable value can make the difference between surviving and shutting up shop.

What is the Screwed Score?

The Screwed Score is calculated as:

Screwed Score = (Rateable Value ÷ Estimated Turnover) × 100

A score of 100 means the rateable value equals the estimated annual turnover — which is already very high. Scores above 110 indicate the nail bar is paying disproportionately large business rates relative to what it likely earns.

✅ Under 95 — Doing alright
🔵 95–109 — A bit screwed
🟡 110–124 — Pretty screwed
🔴 125+ — Royally screwed

Where does the data come from?

Rateable values are drawn from the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) business rates dataset, which is published as open data by the UK government. Turnover estimates are based on industry averages for nail bars of different sizes in different locations.

Data is provided for informational purposes only and is not official financial or legal advice. Rateable values change with revaluations — the most recent was in 2023.

Why nail bars?

The nail bar industry has grown rapidly in the UK over the past decade, but many nail bars operate on very slim margins. High-street locations with elevated rateable values can result in annual rates bills that represent a significant proportion of turnover — leaving owners feeling, well, screwed.
